Cappadocia is located in eastern Anatolia, in the center of modern-day Türkiye. Its terrain consists of a plateau plain 1,000 meters above sea level, interspersed with volcanic peaks, and Mount Argeis, near Caesarea, which is the highest of the highlands, as its summit is located at an altitude of 3,916 metres. The borders of Cappadocia are not exactly clear, especially towards the west. To the south, the Taurus Mountains form the border with Cilicia and separate it from the Mediterranean Sea. To the west, Cappadocia is bordered by the provinces of Lyconia to the southwest and Galatia to the northwest. The coastal mountain range of the Black Sea separates Cappadocia from the Pontus and the Black Sea to the north, while it is bordered to the east by the Upper Euphrates River, before it turns to the southeast to empty into Mesopotamia and the Armenian Highlands.
